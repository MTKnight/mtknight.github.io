Never Give Up
Never Surrender

I'll be honest. I feel ridiculous writing this journal right now. I don't really like sharing personal things, especially about my family. But, to maintain the spirit of this journal, and honestly in an attempt to feel better, I'm going to try to talk about what's going on with my vaguely.

First, my daughter is ill. She has odd hours which translates into sleep-deprivation for me. On-top of that, my classes are becoming more involved and difficult—especially my practicum. I discovered a huge security risk which is now causing delays as the company I'm volunteering for addresses it. Unfortunately, I'm not the only one struggling with school. The pre-teen has been having some issues that I'm not sure how to help her. I know we'll get through it, but my heart just aches for her.

Oh, and we had major car troubles that seriously dinged us financially, because why wouldn't that happen now?

Elder Uchtdorf with the Assist

All that said, there is *always* more good to be had than not. It may be cynical; but, when it feels like life is piling on, a small sarcastic part of my brain quips that Job and Ezekiel would laugh in my face if I tried whining about these things to them. That said, I know that just because things could be worst doesn't invalidate how I'm feeling, but it does offer perspective. It also offers a reminder that there is someone that Job and Ezekiel knew, that I know, that understands— the Savior.

jUnit


My favorite thing about coding is the opportunity for efficiency. I love being able to create protocols or rules that dictate behavior instead of having to write it out everytime. jUnit is fully onboard with that kind of thinking. jUnit testing is about checking the functionality of the code at the dev level— testing each piece individually. It is about quality assurance. Using jUnit tests automates this process. Which, if we're honest, is the most boring part of coding. So WIN!
