Icebreaker

"Hello, everyone! I am a senior Computer Information Technology major from Henderson, Nevada, USA which is in the Las Vegas Valley. This puts me in the Pacific Time Zone, an hour behind BYU-I. My professional interests are Project Management and Business Analytics. My personal interests are reading books, playing games, and learning languages. I'm particularly partial to fantasy novels when reading for fun. I don't discriminate with medium when it comes to games. I play both board and video games and play on a variety on consoles. While I love the study of linguistics in general, I am partial to learning minority Western European languages; I'm especially in love with Jèrriais."


It was fun to talk with other IT people about my non-IT interests. Most people outside of the Channel Islands have never heard of Jèrriais, so it was a special delight to spread the good word. I don't really see a professional path for me, but it would be an absolute dream to use my degree to help preserve Jèrriais and similiar struggling minority languages. linguistics is such a specilized academic field, it's really hard to make a living of any kind without being a savant, but it would be a dream come true to be able to immerse myself beyond being a hobbyist.



Class Preparation


For my IDE, I've chosen to use Eclipse. I used Eclipse in CIT 260 which is the foundation of everything I know about java. I have to admit I'm really nervous about this class, so I really don't want to relearn an IDE on top of the everything else. I'm pretty comfortable with Visual Studio Code, but it's my understanding that using it for java takes some forcing. I doubt I have the skill to do that. Our teacher speaks highly of IntelliJ, and I'm really tempted to switch as a result; but I'm worried that I'll just make everything more complicated for myself.
